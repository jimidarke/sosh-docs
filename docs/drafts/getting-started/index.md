# Getting started with your Guardian

This track takes you from an unopened box to your first working shelf label — and a team ready to use it. Plan for about an hour. Each lesson builds on the one before it, so do them in order. By the end, your Guardian will be on your store's network, your products will flow in from your point-of-sale system (POS), and a real price will be live on a real shelf.

## The lessons

1. [How Sovereign Shelf works](a1-how-it-works.md) — a 5-minute overview of the tags, the Beacons (the small wireless bridges that talk to your tags), and the Guardian — and what to expect on day one.
2. [Set up the hardware](a2-set-up-the-hardware.md) — plug in the Guardian and the Beacons. There are no settings to enter, and tags are found automatically.
3. [Sign in to your Guardian console](a3-sign-in.md) — open the Guardian console — the dashboard you open in a web browser on your store's network — and take a quick tour.
4. [Connect your product data](a4-connect-your-product-data.md) — link your POS so products and prices flow in on their own.
5. [Bind your first tag](a5-bind-your-first-tag.md) — bind (link a tag to a product) and watch your first live price appear on the shelf.
6. [First-day settings](a6-first-day-settings.md) — a few quick settings worth doing on day one.
7. [Train your team](a7-train-your-team.md) — pair a store handheld, add your staff, and hand them their own short guide.

## What you'll need

- Your Guardian and your Beacons, from the box.
- The quick-start card from the box — it has your console address and sign-in details.
- A computer or tablet with a web browser, connected to your store's network.
- Your store's router, with a spare network port for the Guardian.
- A power outlet for the Guardian and one near each Beacon.
- Your POS sign-in details — you'll need them for lesson 4.

!!! tip "Setting up before opening day?"
    You don't need the store to be open — the whole track works before your doors do. Lesson 7 is easiest when your staff are on site, so you can put a handheld in their hands right away.
